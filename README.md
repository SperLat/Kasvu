# Kasvu

An open-source leadership philosophy for cultivating resilience, growth, and emergent greatness.

Kasvu is a leadership philosophy built on a simple conviction: greatness cannot be imposed into existence like a statue. It must be cultivated like a garden. It must grow from within.

Kasvu exists to help leaders, teams, and organizations unlock the potential they already contain. Rather than forcing people and systems into rigid forms, it invites us to create the conditions in which resilience, character, and excellence can emerge. 

This repository is the open-source home for Kasvu: its philosophy, pillars, frameworks, practical templates, calibration scenarios as well as AI agentic information.

## Why Kasvu

Too much leadership is still shaped by the logic of sculpture:
define the perfect form, remove everything that does not fit, and optimize for control.

Kasvu offers another path.

It is built around the belief that healthy systems grow from within. Leadership, in this view, is not primarily the act of imposing shape, but the discipline of cultivating conditions: clarity, care, resilience, experimentation, and direction.

Kasvu is not about replicating greatness.
It is about cultivating resilience so that greatness can emerge as a consequence.

This means, Kasvu is not only for leadership and corporations, it offers a new perspective on how to grow and tackle life scenarios in a socratic yet practical way.

## Core metaphor

Kasvu is rooted in a distinction between two ways of shaping the world:

- **Sculpting**: force, symmetry, replication, control.
- **Cultivating**: observation, guidance, resilience, emergence.

A statue can be impressive.
A garden can become alive.

This distinction is at the heart of Kasvu’s view of leadership, culture, and organizational design.

## The pillars
Kasvu is built around six interconnected pillars.

These pillars are not a checklist. They form an ecosystem of principles that work together to support healthy growth.

-Culture and engagement
-Development
-Process improvement
-Experimentation
-Quality
-Prospective thinking

Each pillar is documented separately and connected back to the broader philosophy.

## How to use Kasvu
Kasvu can be used in different ways depending on your context.

For leaders
Use Kasvu to reflect on how you shape culture, make decisions, guide teams, and create the conditions for internal growth during times of massive growth.

For teams
Use the frameworks and templates in this repository to run retrospectives, 1:1s, planning sessions, and strategy conversations.

For organizations
Use Kasvu as a lens for designing systems that are resilient, human-centered, and capable of evolving without losing coherence. Investing in people will retain the core of your culture without increasing costs and allowing to bring external top talent rather than diluting your culture at expense of "lack of internal talent".

## Authoring philosophy
This repository is meant to remain practical, readable, and alive.

When writing or contributing to Kasvu materials, prefer:

Clarity over jargon

Practice over abstraction

Reflection over prescription

Living systems over rigid doctrine

Resilience over performative perfection

Kasvu should feel usable in real conversations, not only inspiring in theory. Should be Socratic and self-aware so that you also grow as you let others grow.

## Authorship and stewardship
Kasvu is a founder-led philosophy and an evolving open-source body of work.

This repository is the place where the philosophy is documented, clarified, expanded into frameworks, and made easier to apply in practice.

As the project grows, stewardship may expand. Its purpose, however, remains the same: to help cultivate the conditions in which people, teams, and systems can grow into their own strength.
