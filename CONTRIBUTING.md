# Contributing to Kasvu

Kasvu is a living philosophy. It is not finished, and it is not meant to be.

The frameworks, templates, and examples in this repository are designed to grow through real-world use, honest reflection, and the contributions of people who have applied these ideas in practice.

This document explains how to contribute in a way that keeps the project coherent, honest, and aligned with the spirit of Kasvu.

---

## Before you contribute

Read these first if you have not already:

- `docs/philosophy.md` — to understand what Kasvu is and what it is not.
- `docs/parable.md` — to understand the core metaphor that shapes everything.
- `LICENSE` — to understand the terms under which this work is shared.

Contributions that are misaligned with the philosophy will not be merged, not because of rigid rules, but because coherence is how a living philosophy stays alive.

---

## What we welcome

### Clarifications and improvements
If something is unclear, imprecise, or could be said better, open an issue or submit a pull request with a suggested improvement and a brief explanation of why it is better.

### New frameworks
If you have applied Kasvu in a real context and developed a practice worth sharing, we want to hear about it. New frameworks should follow the structure in `frameworks/` and include a clear purpose, a description of when to use it, and which pillars it engages.

### New templates
Practical, copy-paste tools that help leaders and teams apply Kasvu in their daily work. Keep them simple and immediately usable.

### Examples and case narratives
Real or illustrative stories of how Kasvu has been applied. These do not need to be polished. Honest and specific is more valuable than perfect and generic.

### Translations
Kasvu was built to travel. If you want to translate materials into another language, open an issue first so we can coordinate and avoid duplicated effort. Translations live in `docs/[language-code]/` and must remain faithful to the original intent.

### Corrections
Factual errors, broken links, outdated references. Always welcome.

---

## What we do not accept

- Content that contradicts the core philosophy without a serious, well-reasoned argument for why the philosophy itself should evolve.
- Jargon-heavy or overly academic writing that makes Kasvu less accessible.
- Frameworks or templates that have not been tested in any real context.
- Content generated entirely by AI and submitted without human review, editing, and personal accountability for its accuracy and alignment.
- Any contribution that violates the terms of the LICENSE.

---

## How to contribute

### For small changes
Typos, wording improvements, broken links: submit a pull request directly with a clear description of what you changed and why.

### For new content
1. Open an issue first and describe what you want to add. Help us understand why this aligns with Kasvu and we will review it.
2. Wait for a response before investing significant time in writing. We want to value your time, effort and eagerness to contribute and that means sometimes taking a bit longer to get back to you.
3. Once aligned, write the content and submit a pull request.
4. Be prepared for feedback and revision. The goal is coherence, not volume.

### For challenges to the philosophy
If you believe something in the core philosophy is wrong or incomplete, open an issue titled "Philosophy challenge:" followed by the specific point you are questioning. Make your argument clearly and specifically. These conversations are welcome and taken seriously. Growth occurs through adversity but also through adapting and evolving.

---

## How contributions are reviewed

Contributions are reviewed against three questions:

1. Is it aligned with the spirit of Kasvu?
2. Does it add something genuinely useful that is not already here?
3. Is it written in a way that a real leader, end-user or team can use without translation?

If the answer to all three is yes, it will likely be merged. If not, you will receive honest feedback explaining why. .

---

## Writing style

Kasvu materials should feel like they were written by a person who has thought carefully about leadership and wants to be useful to other people who are doing the same while at the same time empowering. You are welcome to write about the philosophy even if you have not held a position of leadership but want to focus on the real life usage of this framework.

When writing for this repository:

- Write in plain English. Avoid jargon unless a term is defined in the glossary.
- Be direct. Say what you mean without excessive qualification.
- Be specific. Concrete examples are worth more than abstract principles.
- Write for a leader reading this at the end of a long day, or someone who wants to be a leader and develop others because they enjoy working with others not for an academic reviewing it at leisure.
- Do not over-structure. Use headings and lists when they help. Do not use them as a substitute for clear thinking.

---

## A note on AI-assisted contributions

AI tools can be useful for drafting, editing, and structuring content. Their use is not prohibited. You may complement them with our AI agent framework as well which is encouraged.

However, any contribution submitted to this repository must be:

- Reviewed and edited by a human who takes personal responsibility for its content.
- Grounded in real experience or genuine understanding, not generated pattern-matching.
- Written in a voice that reflects actual thought and lessons.

If a contribution reads like it was generated and submitted without meaningful human engagement, it will not be merged but you may be encouraged to circle back and complement it or expand it so that it can better adjust to our scope.

---

## Attribution

If your contribution is merged, your name or handle will be visible in the repository's commit history. You may also be acknowledged in release notes for significant contributions.

By submitting a contribution, you agree that it will be shared under the same CC BY-NC-SA 4.0 license that governs this repository.

---

## Questions

If you are unsure about anything before contributing, open an issue and ask. A short conversation upfront saves everyone time. We want to spread Kasvu's philosophy and learnings to others, as well as listening to your own stories.
