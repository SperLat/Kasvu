# The Origin of Kasvu

## Where it began

I grew up in the countryside of Costa Rica, walking amongst cattle and palm trees that produced palm oil and jobs for the farmers. I was lucky to use a computer at the age of six, but I also saw the inequalities of living three hours away from a hospital with no bus to take you there and no car in the garage.

As I grew older I became a member of non-profit organizations. I felt I was doing my part, but I also experienced firsthand what bureaucracy does to simple problems: it makes them permanent.

## The problem I was trying to solve

I saw people conform. They did not see anything broken. The society around them had not built the conditions for people to seek their own growth or to lead change. It had taught them to follow the status quo and settle for the minimum.

That bothered me in a way I could not ignore.

## The turning point

I moved to the capital and saw the same structural problems at a different scale. I had studied education, French, and English, and was beginning a law career. The education gaps and career inequalities I had seen in the countryside were not local problems. They were systemic ones.

I stopped waiting for someone else to address them.

## How the philosophy took shape

During this period I had the opportunity to start a small non-profit organization. That is where I began building the foundation of what would become Kasvu, particularly the pillars of Prospective, Quality, and Culture and Engagement. The rest followed as I grew as a professional and as a person.

Reading authors like Simon Sinek, who helped me understand why purpose must precede everything else, and John Maxwell, who shaped how I thought about leadership as a multiplier rather than a title, gave me a framework for questioning my own experiences and putting them into practice. But also reading the poems of Li Bao and the thoughts of Schopenhauer as I was embracing my artistic side made me challenge how those frameworks were communicated.

Kasvu was not designed. It was lived, tested, and gradually understood through a vivid ecosystem that was never meant to be rigid.

## Why Kasvu

Kasvu means growth in Finnish. It is a name that is meaningful without being heavy, and the Finnish education system is one of the most respected in the world. But everyone also knows that system cannot be copy-pasted into any country or any organization.

Kasvu was born under that same premise. You cannot grow leaders based on a checklist. Growth as an individual is not a matter of completing a list of items and calling it done. It must adapt. It must fit the person, the team, the season, and the soil. It requires patience, honest observation, and the willingness to tend what is growing rather than impose what you imagined.

## Why open source

Kasvu has been used to help and impact directly or indirectly over 3,000 individuals across a 20-year span. Across remote-only companies, fast-paced startups, and corporations navigating hyper-growth. I have personally worked alongside more than 100 individuals who grew into leaders, each one fine-tuning what Kasvu is today in their own way and journey.

I want more organizations to take those learnings and use them. Because individuals are what make your culture. And sometimes, when organizations face pressure and make difficult decisions, the human side of those decisions takes a toll that does not show up in the numbers or the graphs until it is too late.

Kasvu exists so that does not have to keep happening.

## Where it stands today

Kasvu is still evolving. With more technology available than ever before, I have made it my goal to put this together and share it in the scalable way the philosophy has always needed.

You are welcome to collaborate, to use it, to help test the AI-assisted tools being built around it, and to meet me in The Grove.

## Where it is going

Kasvu started as an idea that became an execution, fine-tuned by experience over two decades. No principle here is written in stone, but each one has been carved by people who walked the forest and left something behind for those who follow, so you do not get lost on your way.

You are one of those people now.
