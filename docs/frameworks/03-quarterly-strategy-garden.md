# Quarterly Strategy Review: The Garden Session

## Purpose

This framework helps a leadership team step back at the end of a quarter and assess not just what was produced, but how the system is growing.

A typical quarterly review asks: did we hit the numbers? The Garden Session asks a harder question: are we building something that can keep growing, or are we just hitting this quarter's targets while quietly weakening the system that produces them?

## When to use it

Use this framework when:

- You are closing a quarter and want more than a metrics review.
- The team has been heads-down and needs to look up and around.
- You sense the organization is optimizing for short-term output at the cost of long-term health.
- You want to make Prospective thinking a structural habit, not an occasional exercise.
- You want the leadership team to think together about the system, not just report on their individual areas.

## What you need

- 2 to 3 hours with the full leadership team.
- A summary of the quarter: key results, significant failures, and notable surprises.
- Honest willingness to name what is not working.

## The session flow

### Part 1: What grew this quarter (30 minutes)

Start with what actually got stronger, not just what got done.

There is a difference between completing work and building capacity. This part of the session looks for evidence of real growth.

Ask the team:

- "What did we learn this quarter that we did not know at the start?"
- "Where did we see the system get stronger, not just busier?"
- "Who grew in a way that surprised you?"
- "What experiment worked, even partially?"

Capture the answers visibly. These are the roots worth protecting.

### Part 2: What did not grow (30 minutes)

This is the harder conversation.

Ask the team to look at what stayed stuck, regressed, or was quietly avoided.

- "What problem came up again this quarter that also came up last quarter?"
- "Where did we add activity but not progress?"
- "What did we say was a priority that the system did not actually treat as one?"
- "Where are we sculpting when we should be cultivating?"

The last question is the most important one in this section. It often surfaces the real strategic tension.

### Part 3: Where the wind blows (30 minutes)

This is the Prospective section of the session.

Ask the team to look outward and forward together.

- "What is changing in our environment that we have not fully named yet?"
- "What are our customers expecting now that they were not expecting six months ago?"
- "What is becoming less relevant that we are still investing in?"
- "What opportunity is becoming visible that we have not moved on yet?"
- "Where does the wind blow to?"

The goal is not to produce a forecast. The goal is to make the team's collective peripheral vision visible so it can inform decisions in the next quarter.

### Part 4: One thing to cultivate (30 minutes)

Based on everything surfaced in the session, the team identifies one thing to deliberately cultivate in the next quarter.

Not a project. Not a metric. A condition.

Examples of conditions worth cultivating:

- Psychological safety in a team that has been operating under pressure.
- Experimentation habits in a function that has become risk-averse.
- Development conversations that have been replaced by operational ones.
- A prospective dialogue practice that does not yet exist.

The team agrees on what cultivating that condition will look like in practice: who will tend it, how they will know it is growing, and what would signal it is being neglected.

### Part 5: Commitments (15 minutes)

Close the session with one commitment per leader.

Not a project plan. One specific thing each person will do differently in the next quarter based on what emerged in this session.

Write them down. Return to them at the start of next quarter's Garden Session.

## Facilitator notes

The most common failure mode of this session is drifting back into metrics. When the conversation returns to numbers, gently redirect: "We will get to the numbers. What I want to stay with for a moment is what the numbers tell us about the health of the system."

The session works best when someone other than the most senior person in the room facilitates. That person's presence tends to shape what others feel safe saying.

If the team struggles to name what did not grow, that is itself important information. A team that cannot name its problems has a Culture and Engagement issue, not a strategy issue.

## Pillars activated

- Pillar 1: Culture and Engagement — honest conversation about what is and is not working.
- Pillar 4: Experimentation — reviewing what was tested and what was learned.
- Pillar 5: Quality — examining whether what was produced actually served the customer.
- Pillar 6: Prospective — reading the environment and orienting toward what is coming.

## Suggested time

| Part | Time |
|---|---|
| What grew this quarter | 30 minutes |
| What did not grow | 30 minutes |
| Where the wind blows | 30 minutes |
| One thing to cultivate | 30 minutes |
| Commitments | 15 minutes |
| **Total** | **2 hours 15 minutes** |
