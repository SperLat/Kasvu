# Pillar 2: Development

## Philosophy

Development is a shared responsibility between the individual, the leader, and the system.

Kasvu treats development as practical, hands-on, and strategic. It is not a side program or a reward for good behavior. It is one of the main ways an organization builds strength from within.

Real development happens when people are given context, challenge, coaching, repetition, and responsibility in the flow of real work.

## Why it matters

A system that does not develop people eventually weakens itself.

When growth depends only on a few strong individuals, the organization becomes fragile. When development becomes part of the system, capability expands, leadership deepens, and resilience compounds over time. People become eager to grow and participate. Growth becomes the main driver.

## Core principles

- Development must be tied to real work and real responsibility, held within a culture where it is safe to struggle, fail, and try again.
- Development without psychological safety is just exposure.
- Leaders are not only operators; they are developers of people.
- Learning should produce more autonomy, judgment, and initiative over time.
- The goal is not dependency on mentors, but multiplication through others.
- Development is an investment in future capability, not a short-term distraction.

## Operating model

Kasvu favors a cascading mentorship model.

People who grow are expected to help others grow. This creates a multiplier effect in which development does not remain concentrated in a few individuals but spreads through the system.

Investment in development should be intentional, not uniform. More energy should go toward people who are proactive, high-performing, and willing to convert opportunity into responsibility. This is not favoritism; it is stewardship of potential.

## The Echo Effect

The objective of development in Kasvu is not only personal improvement.

It is to create entrepreneurial leaders who become developers of others. Growth should echo outward. One person becomes stronger, helps another person become stronger, and over time the organization gains depth rather than just volume.

This is the Echo Effect.

## How this pillar connects

Development deepens Culture and Engagement because people feel seen, stretched, and trusted.

It supports Process Improvement because better-developed people redesign work more intelligently. It strengthens Experimentation because people become more capable of trying, learning, and adapting. It improves Quality because skill and judgment rise together. It feeds Prospective thinking because stronger people are better equipped to interpret change and prepare for what is coming.

Without Development, growth stalls. With it, the system renews itself from within.
